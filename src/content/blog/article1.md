## Start With The Reader

A portfolio is easier to improve when it has a clear audience. Recruiters, founders, hiring managers, and technical peers all scan for different signals.

Use the home page to answer three questions quickly:

- Who are you?
- What kind of work do you do?
- Where can someone learn more or contact you?

## Keep The Content Maintainable

This template keeps structured data in TypeScript files under `src/data`. That makes names, contact links, CV entries, highlights, and asset paths easy to update without searching through every component.

## Use The Blog As Proof

Blog posts are a good place to show how you think. Short posts about trade-offs, debugging notes, architecture decisions, and lessons learned often communicate more than a long list of technologies.
